# Split-mu Road Identification and Vehicle Stability Control

## One-line thesis

Left-right friction asymmetry produces yaw moments that require wheel-level or side-level friction representation.

## Problem / Gap

Single global friction cannot reproduce Split-μ braking or turning response.

## Method

Identify Split-μ road condition and use it for stability control.

## Key Results

This project must evaluate left/right μ mismatch through yaw rate, lateral velocity, and wheel-speed rollout errors.

## Assumptions

Yaw response and wheel-speed asymmetry are measurable.

## Limitations / Failure Modes

Control-oriented identification may not directly produce long-horizon simulation model.

## Reusable Ingredients

Split-μ scenario design; yaw moment consistency.

## Open Questions

How to encode diagonal or patchy μ mismatch beyond left/right split.
